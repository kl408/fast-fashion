What is FAST FASHION, anyway?

Our Mission

With millions of trendy, affordable pieces storming the fashion industry today, many consumers focus on perfecting their closet. Fast fashion has steadily become a norm yet people are not aware of what fast fashion is.

What is fast fashion?

Fast fashion is inexpensive rapidly-produced clothing that follows the latest trends. Fashion brands such as Forever 21, Gap Inc., H&M, Uniqlo, Urban Outfitters, and Zara are well known establishments which hold products that are a result of fast fashion.


We need to emphasize the impact of fast fashion environmentally and start raising awareness that consumers can influence famous brands and their practices.


Labor

What labor conditions are imposed upon workers?

Article from Green America

Labor Concernsclose

Thousands of workers in sourcing firms located throughout Asia face brutal hours and working conditions to meet the demands of fashion consumers. Workers are often forced to overwork and face low pay.

Most workers are reported to be women working with machine operators and checkers while management positions are male-dominated. This hierarchy of power has made it difficult for women to report physical and mental harm. They are also susceptible to sexual abuse.

Environmental impact

What environmental concerns arise from fast fashion?

Article from WRI

Environmental Impactclose

Most consumers are unaware of the resources needed and process behind clothing production. Creating basic essentials such as cotton shirts and jeans require thousands of liters of water and produce as much greenhouse gases as most vehicles do on long journeys.

Several garments of clothing and fabric are left in landfills everyday. According to the World Resources Institute, nearly 83 billion kilograms of clothing are disposed annually. Clothing made out of non-biodegradable fabric can last for two centuries.
